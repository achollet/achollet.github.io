      <h3 style="color: #F0F0F0; margin-bottom: 2rem;">📄 PhD Thesis</h3>
      
      <div style="background: #1A1F3A; border-radius: 8px; padding: 2rem; margin-bottom: 2rem; border-left: 4px solid #FF2E5C;">
        <h4 style="color: #F0F0F0; margin-bottom: 1rem;">
          <a href="https://theses.hal.science/tel-01179825/" style="color: #FF2E5C; text-decoration: none;">
            Étude expérimentale et théorique de la fragmentation de Composés Organiques Volatils pour des applications environnementales
          </a>
        </h4>
        <p style="color: #8B8B8B; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;"><strong>Alexis Chollet</strong> (2015)</p>
        <p style="color: #8B8B8B; margin-bottom: 1rem;"><strong>University:</strong> Université Paris Sud - Paris XI</p>
        <p style="color: #8B8B8B; line-height: 1.6;">
          This thesis focuses on the experimental and theoretical study of fragmentation of organic molecules. A new non-thermal plasma reactor was built and a statistical fragmentation model (Microcanonical Metropolis Monte Carlo) was adapted to describe the fragmentation of CnHm species. The non-thermal plasma is created by a nanosecond high-voltage pulse (100 kV) with a very steep rise front (15-20 kV.ns⁻¹), allowing generation of large diffuse plasma volume and high densities of reactive species capable of reacting with organic molecules.
        </p>

          <a href="https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/6894610/" style="color: #FF2E5C; text-decoration: none;">
            On the Influence of Anode Composition on the Pattern of a Nanosecond Diffuse Discharge at Atmospheric Pressure
          </a>
        </h4>
        <p style="color: #8B8B8B; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;"><strong>A. Chollet, P. Jeanney, S. Pasquiers, P. Tardiveau</strong> (2014)</p>
        <p style="color: #8B8B8B; margin-bottom: 1rem;"><strong>Journal:</strong> IEEE Transactions on Plasma Science</p>
        <p style="color: #8B8B8B; line-height: 1.6;">
          A diffuse discharge is generated in atmospheric air under a nanosecond range overvoltage. The influence of the anode composition on the diffuse pattern is studied and images of the electrical streamer pattern are analyzed to understand how different anode materials affect the discharge characteristics.
        </p>
